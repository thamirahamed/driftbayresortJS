FA HIEN CAVES

Kalutara

Fa Hien Cave, also known as Pahiyangala Cave, is a historically significant site in Sri Lanka. It has a history of human habitation dating back 37,000 years, with evidence of prehistoric tools and ancient fireplaces. The cave is also associated with Buddhism, as it is said to have hosted the renowned Buddhist monk Fa Hien during his pilgrimage to India in the 5th century CE. Over the centuries, the cave has been adorned with man-made structures, including a reclining Buddha statue and a stupa.

WHALE WATCHING

Mirissa

Sri Lanka is a premier destination for whale watching enthusiasts, offering a remarkable opportunity to witness these majestic marine creatures in their natural habitat. The country's strategic location in the Indian Ocean makes it a hotspot for diverse marine life, including blue whales, sperm whales, and various species of dolphins. Tour operators offer guided boat trips, where visitors can observe these gentle giants and playful dolphins up close.

YALA NATIONAL PARK

Hambantota

Yala National Park, covering an area of 979 square kilometers, is not only Sri Lanka's second largest but also its most renowned national park. Covering an extensive area of approximately 978 square kilometers, this park boasts a remarkable diversity of ecosystems, including dense forests, arid plains, freshwater lakes, and brackish lagoons. Within its boundaries, visitors have the chance to encounter a rich variety of wildlife, including Sri Lankan elephants, leopards, sloth bears, and countless bird species.
